I am a PhD student at the Institute for Quantum Computing and the Department of Physics and Astronomy at the University of Waterloo. My research focuses on the fundamental physics of quantum many-body systems, particularly in exploring thermalization, ergodicity, chaos, recurrences, and information scrambling in non-equilibrium system. I am also deeply interested in how these phenomena intersect with quantum information science, especially for applications in metrology, sensing, and scalable quantum computing.

I completed my MSc in Physics at the Institute for Quantum Computing in 2023, where I worked under the supervision of [Prof. Shohini Ghose](https://www.wlu.ca/academics/faculties/faculty-of-science/faculty-profiles/shohini-ghose/index.html) and [Prof. Robert Mann](https://uwaterloo.ca/physics-astronomy/profile/rbmann) on my thesis titled "[Dynamical stability and chaos in closed kicked systems](https://scholar.google.com/citations?view_op=view_citation&hl=en&user=CChmN-YAAAAJ&citation_for_view=CChmN-YAAAAJ:W7OEmFMy1HYC)". 

Prior to that, I earned my BTech in Mechanical Engineering from IIEST Shibpur, Kolkata, in 2021 and gained early research experience as a summer student at IISER Kolkata. Alongside my research, I am committed to teaching, outreach, and fostering an inclusive academic community.
